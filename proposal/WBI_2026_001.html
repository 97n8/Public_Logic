Device Lending Program Case Workspace

MBI Connected & Online Device Lending Program
Prepared for: Wachusett Business Incubator
Prepared by: PublicLogic LLC
Date: January 14, 2026

Purpose

Wachusett Business Incubator is receiving devices through the MBI Connected & Online Device Lending Program. These devices carry clear requirements for tracking, quarterly reporting, incident handling, and ownership continuity through at least December 31, 2031.

This proposal establishes a structured, live case workspace that embeds governance designed to survive real life. The workspace keeps the program compliant and visible over time, even as staff, priorities, and circumstances change.

Objectives:
  • Always know the status and location of every device
  • Produce required reports from live records, not reconstruction
  • Surface issues early, while they remain manageable
  • Maintain continuity through staff transitions
  • Sustain compliance across the full required period

The system is intentionally lightweight and proportionate to a regional organization. It reduces administrative burden rather than adding to it.

What This Is (and What It Is Not)

This is:

  • A one-time build of a durable program workspace
  • A single authoritative source for all device records
  • A structure with built-in tracking, reminders, and reporting logic
  • Fully owned and operated by WBI after handoff

This is not:

  • New software or a subscription platform
  • An IT system requiring ongoing vendor support
  • Retained consulting or external oversight

What the Case Workspace Provides

Live Program Monitoring

Real-time visibility into device lifecycle events: issued, returned, overdue, retired, or flagged. Nothing relies on memory or informal notes.

Reporting Continuity

Reporting schedules and required metrics are built into the workspace. Counts and summaries are generated from current data, not recreated at reporting time.

Early Warning and Correction

Overdue devices, missing documentation, incidents, and upcoming deadlines surface early, when corrective action is still straightforward.

Core Components

  1. Master Device Register
    Central record of serial numbers, status, loan history, condition notes, and disposition.
  2. Request and Approval Workflow
    Documented process so decisions are visible and auditable.
  3. Borrower Agreements and Checklists
    Plain-language forms outlining responsibilities and return expectations.
  4. Incident and Loss Protocol
    Clear steps for documentation, notification, and resolution.
  5. Reporting and Archive Folder
    Consistent storage of quarterly summaries and narratives.
  6. Staff Operations Guide
    Step-by-step instructions that survive staff turnover.
  7. QR Device Labels (Optional)
    Quick access to device records using WBI-controlled links, with no borrower data exposed.
