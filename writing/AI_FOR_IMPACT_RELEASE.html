PublicLogic and Polimorphic Announce Launch of AI for Impact Pilot for Massachusetts Municipalities

A governance-led, people-centered pilot focused on continuity, authority, and institutional memory

FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE
MMA Connect 351 Conference
Boston, MA — January 20, 2026

Massachusetts — PublicLogic, in partnership with Polimorphic, today announced the launch of the AI for Impact Pilot, a no-cost, voluntary pilot for Massachusetts cities and towns experiencing staff turnover, audit pressure, or capacity strain.

In every municipality, the same invisible work happens. Experienced staff inherit unwritten rules. They remember past decisions. They bridge gaps created by turnover and incomplete documentation. They hold the institution together.

When they leave, municipalities discover how much responsibility was never actually held by systems. It was held by people.

The AI for Impact Pilot is designed to reverse this pattern. It is people-centered and governance-led. The pilot does not begin with technology. It begins by moving institutional burden out of individuals and into durable structure through clear authority, documented decisions, explicit processes, and defensible records.

Only after those foundations are in place are AI-assisted tools introduced to support routing, documentation, and continuity within clearly defined governance boundaries. AI assists staff. It does not replace judgment or authority.

Pilot Details

Priority consideration is given to municipalities under 25,000 residents.

The pilot runs for twelve weeks, at no cost, with no obligation to continue.

“People are already doing the hardest work. Technology only helps when the structure around it is clear. This pilot puts governance first so AI reduces burden instead of accelerating existing problems.”

“For decades, public institutions have relied on talented people quietly holding together work that was never clearly held by systems. When authority and expectations live in the structure, people can finally just do the work. That is what this pilot builds.”

“This is about protecting institutional memory before it walks out the door. When decisions, authority, and precedent are structurally documented, continuity does not depend on who stays late or remembers the history. The institution can stand on its own.”

About PublicLogic

PublicLogic is a Massachusetts-based governance consulting firm that helps municipalities stop relying on people to hold together work that should be held by systems. The firm combines practitioner experience in municipal administration with clinical expertise in institutional and behavioral systems—an approach that addresses both the structural and human dimensions of how local government actually functions under pressure.

For example, we map undocumented decision authority, turn implicit handover practices into explicit protocols, and build defensible records that survive staff transitions—freeing experienced employees to focus on service rather than constantly filling gaps.

PublicLogic focuses on Massachusetts communities under 25,000 residents. Learn more at publiclogic.org.
