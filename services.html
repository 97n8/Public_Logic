Services

Scoped, practitioner-led governance support that produces durable artifacts and interfaces towns can own, defend, and operate independently.

What towns actually receive

PublicLogic’s work produces artifacts, interfaces, and operating clarity that towns can use without us present.

These outputs are not theoretical. They are the working surfaces where authority, responsibility, and records are actually carried day to day.

Governance artifacts

These are the documents and records that make authority and responsibility explicit.

They commonly include:

  • Authority and decision maps that show who can decide what, under which conditions
  • Statutory workflow documentation tied to actual roles, not idealized charts
  • Record ownership and retention structures aligned with law and practice
  • Transfer-ready procedures that allow work to survive staff turnover
  • Governance notes and rationale that explain why structures exist, not just how, so decisions remain defensible after turnover

These artifacts are written to be read by successors, auditors, counsel, and boards. Not just by the people who helped create them.

Working interfaces and systems

Where governance must live inside systems, we help structure and configure usable interfaces that staff actually work in.

This may include:

  • Intake forms and case structures that reflect statutory requirements
  • Internal views that make responsibility, status, and next steps visible
  • Record structures that reduce reliance on memory and inboxes
  • Simple automation that supports, rather than replaces, human judgment

We work inside existing municipal environments whenever possible. The goal is not novelty. The goal is clarity and survivability.

The UI is not the product. It is the surface that carries the structure.

Training and operational grounding

Artifacts and interfaces only hold if people understand how to use them under pressure.

Training focuses on:

  • How authority actually operates in the system
  • What staff are responsible for, and what they are not
  • How records, decisions, and timelines are meant to move
  • How to operate without improvising governance on the fly

Training is role-specific and grounded in real workflows. It is designed to reduce cognitive load so governance does not depend on memory or improvisation.

How the work is carried

PublicLogic’s work is led directly by the principals and accountable to them.

Nathan and Allison remain actively involved in governance design, risk interpretation, and structural decisions. Staff support implementation, documentation, configuration, and training under direct supervision.

This model ensures:

  • Governance decisions are not delegated away from accountability
  • Behavioral constraints are integrated into structural design
  • Artifacts remain consistent with doctrine and statute
  • Towns are not handed work created by people without authority to make it

Staff extend capacity. They do not replace judgment.

What makes the artifacts different

PublicLogic artifacts are designed to do one thing: hold responsibility without requiring personal sacrifice.

They are:

  • Explicit rather than implied
  • Bounded rather than expansive
  • Transferable rather than personalized
  • Written to survive scrutiny, turnover, and time

If an artifact requires a specific person to interpret or defend it, it is incomplete.

A quiet but important boundary

We do not ship “framework documents” and walk away.

We also do not leave towns dependent on us to explain what we built.

The work is complete when staff can operate the structures confidently, leadership can defend them publicly, and successors can inherit them without re-learning the past.

What We Keep Focused

  • No software platforms or product sales
  • No large-scale culture or training programs
  • No day-to-day operations or staffing takeovers
  • No assumption of formal authority or decision-making roles

We stay grounded in practical governance support that respects institutional limits and real human capacity.
