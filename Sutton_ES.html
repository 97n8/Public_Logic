VAULT for Sutton – Executive Modernization Brief

A practical, modern, and sustainable framework for municipal operations

This is a working brief, not a sales pitch. It is meant to give you a clear picture of how VAULT could support Sutton using the tools you already own, before any formal discovery work or commitments.

1. The challenge Sutton is facing

Sutton operates at the level of a modern city while staffed like a small Massachusetts town. You maintain a strong bond rating and a multi million dollar budget while navigating:

  • Staff at or near capacity
  • Increasing state and federal compliance requirements
  • Grants that require detailed documentation
  • Residents who expect online services and transparency
  • Institutional knowledge that lives in long serving staff

Across the Commonwealth this combination often results in:

  • Burnout and turnover
  • Compliance exposure
  • Longer service times and resident frustration
  • Scrambles to reconstruct documentation for audits, grants, and public records requests

Many critical workflows still rely on manual routines and institutional memory rather than durable systems.

2. What VAULT is, in plain terms

VAULT, Verified Automation and Unified Logic for Towns, standardizes routines, documentation, and compliance across departments.

VAULT is not a new software system. It runs inside the tools Sutton already uses:

  • Google Workspace
  • CivicPlus and CivicCMS
  • Munis
  • Existing departmental tools

PublicLogic designs the workflows and compliance logic. Polimorphic provides the lightweight automation layer that integrates with your existing systems.

Concrete example

In a comparable Massachusetts community the same framework reduced routine posting work and created predictable workflows that continued functioning during staff transitions.

Prepared by Nathan Boudreau, MPA, MCPPO and Dr. Allison Weiss Rothschild, PublicLogic LLC

3. Why this matters now for Sutton

Sutton is in a constructive window:

  • Institutional knowledge is still intact
  • Staff capacity is stretched but functioning
  • Your technology stack is solid but not unified
  • Several state grants can help offset modernization costs

The best time to build durable workflows is before key retirements or turnover.

5. How VAULT works – two applied scenarios

Public Works – VAULT FIX and ServiceTrack

  • Requests route automatically by type and priority
  • Superintendent sees all items in one place
  • Crew update status in the field
  • Labor captured through VAULT TIMETRACK
  • Conditions feed into VAULT CIP
  • Residents updated automatically

Clerk's Office – VAULT CLERK

  • Open Meeting Law postings become predictable
  • Agendas and minutes follow a standard intake path
  • Retention rules enforce automatically
  • Staff receive reminders
