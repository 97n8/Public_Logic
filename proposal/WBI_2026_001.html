RE: MBI Connected & Online Device Lending Program – CASE Workspace Proposal

Dear Mr. Carlberg:

The Wachusett Business Incubator is receiving devices through the Massachusetts Broadband Institute Connected & Online Device Lending Program, administered by the Massachusetts Broadband Institute under the Massachusetts Technology Collaborative.

Devices distributed through this program carry defined requirements for inventory tracking, quarterly reporting, incident documentation, and ownership continuity through at least December 31, 2031, regardless of staff changes, leadership transitions, or program evolution.

This proposal establishes a structured, live CASE workspace that embeds governance designed to survive real life. The workspace ensures that the Device Lending Program remains compliant, visible, and manageable over time without reliance on informal knowledge or individual memory.

Program Objectives

  • Maintain continuous visibility into the status and location of every device
  • Produce required quarterly and closeout reports from live records, not reconstruction
  • Surface issues early, while corrective action remains straightforward
  • Preserve continuity through staff turnover or role changes
  • Sustain compliance for the full required ownership and reporting period

The system is intentionally lightweight and proportionate to a regional organization. It reduces administrative burden rather than adding to it.

What This Is and What It Is Not

This is:

  • A one-time build of a durable Device Lending Program workspace
  • A single authoritative source for all device records and documentation
  • A structure with embedded tracking, reminders, and reporting logic
  • Fully owned and operated by WBI following handoff

This is not:

  • New software or a subscription platform
  • An IT system requiring ongoing vendor support
  • Retained consulting or external oversight

Information Structure

Device_Lending_Case_Workspace

├── Requests_and_Approvals

One source of truth. No parallel systems. Governance that survives real life.

Timeline

  • Week 1: Build registers, forms, agreements, and workspace structure
  • Week 2: Test workflows, validate reporting outputs, and complete handoff

Total duration: Two weeks

Cost and Terms

Fair market value is $7,200. The professional fee is waived for this engagement. There are no subscriptions, no future obligations, and no exclusivity.

Ownership and Responsibility

Upon handoff, the Wachusett Business Incubator assumes full ownership of all files, links, and records. PublicLogic retains no access, control, or ongoing role. All compliance decisions and program responsibility rest solely with WBI.
