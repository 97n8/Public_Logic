Summary

Public_Logic, Capital Strategic Solutions (CSS), and Polimorphic propose a municipal-first partnership to launch a Capital Improvement Plan (CIP) program with a CSS client town. CSS leads delivery and rollout. Public_Logic supplies templates, process standards, and meeting-ready materials. Polimorphic provides the digital platform for intake, scoring, dashboards, and public transparency. The town owns the plan and all data.

This final version keeps the full visual language and structure you liked: hero gradient, partnership triangle, sticky TOC, cards, and print-safe styles — now with a crisp “Why It Matters” section and partner logos banner.

Partnership Model (Portable & Repeatable)

  • CSS leads implementation: intake, workshops, scoring, affordability review, board packet prep, and public communications.
  • Public_Logic supplies standards: templates, process playbooks, calendars, and checklists that travel town to town.
  • Polimorphic provides the platform: digital intake, workflow, dashboards, and public-facing transparency pages.
  • Town owns outcomes: final plan, data, schedules, and adoption decisions stay with the municipality.
  • Shared learning: anonymized lessons improve future rollouts — a win-win-win.

Platform Role (Polimorphic)

What Polimorphic Powers

  • Project intake forms with validation
  • Role-based workflow and notifications
  • Dashboards for finance and leadership
  • Public transparency pages (read-only)

Data & Ownership

  • Municipality owns all data and exports
  • Routine CSV/JSON/PDF exports for portability
  • Access controls and basic audit logs
  • No vendor lock-in: templates travel

Why It Matters — The Problems We Solve Together

The Problems

  • Fragmented modernization: Disconnected tools, inconsistent processes, incomplete data.
  • Grants without staying power: Projects lack standards, training, and governance.
  • Staff overload: Paper or siloed workflows reduce capacity and morale.
  • Limited visibility for boards: Decisions made without a consolidated data picture.
  • Taxpayer inefficiency: Priorities and progress unclear to residents.

The Partnership Solution

  • Public_Logic: Standards, playbooks, calendars, and repeatable governance.
  • CSS: Implementation leadership, facilitation, board-ready materials.
  • Polimorphic: Digital intake, workflow, dashboards, public transparency pages.

The Outcome

  • Tailored modernization: Configured to local law, capacity, and budget.
  • Grant-backed delivery: Evidence and exports that satisfy funders.
  • Staff value & retention: Training-first rollout; staff become process owners.
  • Centralized data ecosystem: A connected, portable record across modules.
  • Taxpayer efficiency: Transparent decisions and faster service.

What the CIP Module Does

  • Delivers a rolling 5–10 year plan with an adoptable Year 1 capital budget.
  • Defines “what counts as capital,” sets thresholds, and standardizes project sheets.
  • Ranks projects with a transparent rubric so boards and residents can see the “why.”
  • Shows affordability: debt capacity, pay-go, grants, and operating impacts.
  • Produces public-facing summaries for meetings and the website.

Process (Simple and Repeatable)

Annual Cycle

  • Fall: project intake and updates
  • Winter: scoring, draft plan, affordability checks
  • Spring: public meetings and adoption
  • Summer: grant work and project tracking

Meeting-Ready Materials

  • Capital policy statement (draft)
  • Project one-pagers (scope, cost, funding, O&M)
  • Ranked list with scoring notes
  • Slide deck for public hearing
  • Website summary and FAQ

Prioritization (Transparent Scoring)

CriterionWhat We Look For
Health & SafetyKnown hazards, code issues, risk of failure
Legal/RegulatoryMandates, time-bound orders or agreements
Asset ConditionInspection results, useful life, deferred maintenance
Service ImpactMaintains core services or expands capacity
External FundingGrants, reimbursements, partner dollars
ReadinessScope clarity, design/permitting status
Operating ImpactStaffing and O&M costs identified early

The rubric is built with department heads and finance so everyone agrees on the rules before ranking.

Success Metrics (How We Know It Worked)

  • Adoption Rate: Town adopts Year 1 capital budget by Spring Town Meeting with no major revisions.
  • Transparency Score: All projects show scoring notes; residents can see “why.”
  • Efficiency Gain: Intake + ranking time reduced 40% in Year 2 vs. pre-CIP.
  • Platform Utilization: 90%+ of intake and scoring in Polimorphic with on-time notifications and reports.
  • Reusability: CSS deploys the combined program to a second town within 6 months using refined templates and configurations.
