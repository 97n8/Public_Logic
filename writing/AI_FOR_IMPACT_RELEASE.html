PublicLogic and Polimorphic Launch “AI for Impact” Pilot

A people-first pilot that helps Massachusetts municipalities modernize by strengthening structure and capacity

January 21, 2026

Massachusetts — PublicLogic, in partnership with Polimorphic, today announced the launch of the AI for Impact Pilot, a no-cost, voluntary, twelve-week initiative open to Massachusetts cities and towns.

Local governments face a familiar challenge. When experienced staff leave, critical knowledge often leaves with them. Past decisions become difficult to trace, precedent fades, and new employees inherit gaps that increase risk, inefficiency, and burnout.

The AI for Impact Pilot takes a different approach. Instead of layering artificial intelligence onto unclear processes, the program starts by making everyday work easier to understand and sustain. Roles are clarified. Decision paths are documented. Records are organized so work can be followed from start to finish.

The pilot does not deploy AI into live production decision-making or replace staff judgment. Any AI support used during the pilot operates in a constrained, internal role and is governed by explicit rules around authority, records, and accountability.

“AI can absolutely help front-line staff and improve service for residents,” said Parth Shah, co-founder and CEO of Polimorphic. “But it only works when expectations are clear and systems are connected. This pilot puts structure first so AI strengthens how municipalities already operate, instead of accelerating existing problems.”

“For too long, organizations have asked people to carry responsibility the institution never clearly held,” said Nathan Boudreau, founder of PublicLogic and former Massachusetts Town Administrator. “When expectations are unclear and decisions aren’t documented, staff compensate with personal judgment and stress. This process fixes that by moving the weight into the system so people aren’t carrying it alone.”
