Executive Summary

PublicLogic™ and Polimorphic propose a practitioner‑built, compliance‑first platform that lets municipalities modernize operations while preserving ownership and control. Our platform partner (e.g., Polimorphic) delivers the resident/staff UX and workflow engine; PublicLogic supplies the standards, governance, and the high‑level logic layer — VAULT™ (policy references, legal holds, retention outcomes) and ARCHIVE™ (immutable evidence packaging and export).

VAULT/ARCHIVE: What Makes This Different

Scenario 1: Legal Challenge Recovery

A 2022 service request is subpoenaed in 2025. With VAULT/ARCHIVE, the system reconstructs the evidence chain in seconds: timestamps, notifications, assignments, GPS‑stamped photos, closure, and an archiveEvidenceId with checksums — exported as a PDF‑A package with manifest.

Scenario 2: Retention on Autopilot

{
  "requestId": "PRR-2025-00031",
  "retentionPolicy": "VAULT-RULE-PRR-06",
  "trigger": "case_closed",
  "duration": "6y",
  "disposition": "export_then_destroy_with_certificate"
}

When the case closes, the retention clock starts. At disposition, the system checks holds, exports, destroys, and issues a certificate — Clerk receives a quarterly report.

Scenario 3: Grant Deliverable Assembly

Generate a grant package with CSV metrics, PDF‑A exemplars, accessibility audits, and a checksum manifest — in minutes, not days.

Bulk Operations & Performance Targets

Batch API Patterns

POST /vault/policy/batch
{ "cases": [ {"caseId":"PRR-2024-001","module":"CLERK","type":"PRR","closedAt":"2024-03-15"} ] }
  • Rate limits: 100 req/min; batch up to 1,000 records per call.
  • Performance targets: policy lookup <200ms (p95); evidence packaging <2s typical; 1K batch <30s; 500‑case export <60s.

Error Handling Scenarios

  • Policy lookup fails: Flag retentionPolicy:"PENDING", banner to staff, retry w/ exponential backoff, alert both teams after 1h.
  • Legal hold on closed case: Mark Closed – Legal Hold, disable deletion, resume on release.
  • GIS fails but urgent: Supervisor override with reason, include in audit trail.
  • Evidence packaging timeout: Return 202 + jobId; poll until export‑complete webhook.
  • Disposition ready but delayed: 90‑day window; default policy executes if no decision.

CLERK Module: Compliance‑Critical Operations

What CLERK Actually Does

Automates the Town Clerk’s office across three high‑risk domains: Public Records Requests (PRR), Licensing, and Open Meeting Law (OML) posting. Tracks statutory clocks, coordinates multi‑department work, and produces a defensible audit trail.

  • PRR: Intake, classify/scope, department routing, redaction workflow, delivery, close → retention & evidence checkpoints.
  • Licensing: Conditional forms by license type; multi‑department approvals; certificate generation with e‑signature; renewal automation; public register.
  • OML Posting: 48‑hour deadline calculator; agenda/packet builder; multi‑channel posting receipts; minutes linkage and archive.

Why It Matters

  • Litigation protection: Timestamped evidence (who did what, when, and why) with archiveEvidenceId references.
  • State compliance: c.66 §10 response clocks; c.30A posting receipts; c.140/c.138 approval chains.
  • Revenue security: Renewal automation prevents revenue leakage on licenses.
  • Public trust: Transparent PRR log, searchable license register, on‑time postings.

Technical Requirements for Polimorphic

  • Multi‑department task routing with per‑task due dates and escalation.
  • Redaction tool for PDFs/images with justification log and versioning.
  • Deadline calculators: PRR 10 business days; OML 48‑hour posting lead; license expirations.
  • Certificate/letter templating with merge fields and e‑signature integration.
  • SHA‑256 checksums on all files; delivery manifests; searchable PRR log.
  • Public registers (licenses, agendas) with CSV export and access controls.

Measurable Outcomes (Targets)

  • PRR: 100% on‑time within statutory window; zero AG complaints.
  • Licensing: 95%+ renewal rate; issuance cycle reduced by >30%.
  • OML: 100% postings ≥48 hours before meeting; complete posting receipts.
  • Quality: Accessibility checks pass (WCAG 2.1 AA); export packages validated quarterly.

PRR Deep Path (Illustrative)

  1. Intake via web/email/phone log → Clock starts (10 business days).
  2. Scope & route tasks to departments; exemption picker guides review.
  3. Redact with justification; deliver package with checksum manifest.
  4. Close case → VAULT retention reference applied; ARCHIVE evidence ID issued.

FIX Module: Resident‑Facing Service Excellence

What FIX Actually Does

Turns DPW into an SLA‑driven, transparent operation. Handles resident service requests (potholes, streetlights, trees, snow, trash) and DPW permits (trench/driveway/road opening) with GIS validation, crew dispatch, and before/after photo evidence.

  • Service Requests: Multi‑channel intake (web, mobile, staff log); SLA tiers; status notifications; closure with photos.
  • DPW Permits: Conditional uploads; multi‑department review; certificate with conditions; inspection & bond release.
  • Analytics: Dashboards, GIS heatmaps, crew performance, grant‑ready metrics.

Why It Matters

  • Speed: 74% faster average resolution (8.3 days → 2.1 days) after pilot standardization.
  • Liability protection: Photo‑backed, timestamped closures defend against claims.
  • Resident trust: Public status and proactive notifications reduce status‑check calls by 80%+.
  • Grant alignment: Metrics and evidence packages satisfy Community Compact IT and MassWorks reporting.

Technical Requirements for Polimorphic

  • GIS integration (address validation, map pin, heatmaps); coordinate capture on mobile.
  • SLA engine with business‑hours calculation and escalations.
  • Mobile crew app: offline support, nav links, photo capture with GPS/timestamp watermark.
  • Attachment handling with checksums; evidence checkpoint on closure.
  • Public portal: anonymous status check, resident history, feedback surveys.
  • Reporting: real‑time dashboards; CSV/JSON exports with SLA flags and coordinates.

Measurable Outcomes (Targets)

  • Tier‑1 (Safety) SLA attainment ≥98% (4‑hour response).
  • Average resolution time ≤3 days for Tier‑2 (Service).
  • Resident satisfaction ≥4.0/5; 50%+ reduction in status‑check calls.
  • Evidence completeness ≥95% (before/after photos present on closures requiring proof).

Core Workflow (Service Request)

  1. Intake (web/mobile/staff) with photo + geocode → ID issued.
  2. Validate GIS; auto‑assign by issue type; set SLA tier.
  3. Crew dispatch; resident notifications at key status changes.
  4. Closure with before/after photos → archiveEvidenceId issued; retention applied (VAULT‑RULE‑SR‑03).
