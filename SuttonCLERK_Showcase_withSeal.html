Plain language law map

Business Certificates run under Massachusetts General Laws chapter 110. The Clerk records a business name and address and issues a certificate after intake and payment.
Public Records follow Massachusetts General Laws chapter 66 and related definitions in chapter 4. The Clerk acknowledges within ten business days and works to complete within twenty five business days when possible for municipal requests.
Vital Records are governed by Massachusetts General Laws chapter 46. The Town may use local printing and a state system to fulfill certified copies.
Open Meeting Law exists under Massachusetts General Laws chapter 30A with regulations. It controls meeting notices and access to records. Local policy can set a shorter timeline for posting approved minutes.
Alcohol Licensing follows Massachusetts General Laws chapter 138 and local rules. Select Board action is required after department review.
Entertainment and similar licenses are covered under Massachusetts General Laws chapter 140. Certain applications require Select Board approval and a public agenda.
Transient Vendors are controlled by Massachusetts General Laws chapter 101. Intake through the Clerk with routing to enforcement when applicable.
Raffles and Bazaars operate under Massachusetts General Laws chapter 271. Intake includes police notice and record keeping.
Junk Dealers are covered by Massachusetts General Laws chapter 140. Applications are scheduled for Select Board consideration.

This showcase uses chapter references to anchor the process. Local bylaws and fees apply. Exact sections and fees are maintained in the living lists and adopted fee schedules.
